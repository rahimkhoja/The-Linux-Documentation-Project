1. Documents submitted for review

  • James Tracy sent in a paper about writing and compiling programs with emacs and the g++ compiler.

2. Updated HOWTOs, FAQs and Guides

  • David Braun published v1.5 of his Disk Encryption HOWTO, which has minor updates.
  • In v1.1.6 of Daniel Nofftz's Athlon Powersaving HOWTO you'll find more info about commandline-hacks and the athcool tool.
  • Claudio Cattazzo and Manuele Rampazzo rewrote the Italian HOWTO. The previous version was rather outdated, so they did a nearly-from-scratch job. Also refer to the Italian Linux Documentation Project over at PLUTO.
  • The SSI-UML HOWTO was removed at the request of the author.
  • Machtelt Garrels submitted updates for the Intro to Linux and Bash for Beginners guides.

3. News in The LDP world

  • New mirrors

    This is the list of mirror sites that joined us this November:

    • http://www.alexbp.ro/ldp in Bucharest, Romania, maintained by Alexandru Bradescu-Popa.
    • http://www.seattle-network.com/linux/ldp in Seattle, Washington, by Joe Morthland.
  • New Linux magazine:

    SSC Publishing, publishers of the Linux Journal, will launch a new monthly printed and online publication called "TUX". It will address new Linux users. More info can be found over at SSC, the announcement is here on the feedback mailinglist.

  • Translation Projects:

    Our French colleagues didn't sit still. Over at http://www.traduc.org you can read about their latest accomplishments, among others a translation of the Samba Authenticated Gateway HOWTO and the Complete System Performance Monitoring Guide.

    The Korean LDP chapter put up a gallery with images and documentation of their CodeFests on the http://kldp.org site.

4. HOWTO contribute to The LDP

The Linux Documentation Project (TLDP) is working on developing free, high quality documentation for the GNU/Linux operating system. If you want to help TLDP, consider joining <discuss-subscribe@en.tldp.org>.

Help us create this newsletter. We need volunteers who follow the community (mailing lists, newsgroups, web channels) and report events related to free software documentation. Write to us at <feedback@en.tldp.org>.

The LDP Weekly News is compiled and edited by Machtelt Garrels and Y Giridhar Appaji Nag with help from several other people.
